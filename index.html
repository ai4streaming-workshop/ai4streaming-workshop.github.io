Overview

Welcome to the 1st Workshop on AI for Streaming at CVPR! Streaming is a $50.11 billion industry where hundreds of millions of users demand everyday high-quality 4K content on different platforms. For computer-generated content (e.g., VR, games, metaverse) we also need to consider the computer graphics and rendering pipelines, and possible on-line user interactions. Computer vision and deep learning have emerged as revolutionary forces for rendering content, image and video compression, enhancement, and quality assessment. From neural codecs for efficient compression to deep learning-based video enhancement and quality assessment, these advanced techniques are setting new standards for streaming quality and efficiency. Moreover, novel neural representations also pose new challenges and opportunities in rendering streamable content, and allowing to redefine computer graphics pipelines and visual content. This workshop focuses on unifying new streaming technologies, computer graphics, and computer vision, from the modern deep learning point of view.

Call for Papers

We welcome papers addressing topics related to efficient image/video processing and neural compression. The topics include:

  • Efficient Deep Learning
  • Model optimization and Quantization
  • Image/video quality assessment
  • Image/video super-resolution and enhancement

  • Generative Models
  • Neural Codecs
  • Real-time Rendering
  • Neural Compression

Challenges üöÄ

We are happy to host the following grand challenges focused on realistic image/video applications.

  • Adaptive Image Super-Resolution
    • A single neural network upscales compressed images with different compression factors.

  • Real-time Video Super-Resolution
    • Upscale videos in real-time at 60FPS on commercial GPUs.

  • UGC Video Quality Assessment
    • Estimate the quality of user-generated content (UGC) videos using efficient neural networks.

  • Event-based Eye Tracking

The top ranked participants will be awarded and invited to describe their solution and to submit to the associated AIS workshop at CVPR 2024. The results of the challenge will be published at AIS 2024 workshop, and in the CVPR 2024 Workshops proceedings.

We also invite you to check the challenges at the New Trends in Image Restoration and Enhancement (NTIRE) workshop.
